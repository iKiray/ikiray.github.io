About Me

Background

I am building this portfolio to document my progress as I learn IT fundamentals, command-line tools, scripting, troubleshooting, networking, and system administration concepts.

My interest in IT comes from a desire to understand how systems work and to solve real-world technical problems. I enjoy the process of learning by doing — building things, breaking them, and figuring out how to fix them.

Why IT?

Technology is everywhere, and I want to be someone who understands it deeply enough to maintain, troubleshoot, and improve it. IT offers a clear career path with continuous learning opportunities, which appeals to me.

Learning Path

I started by exploring basic command-line tools and scripting. From there, I began learning about networking, operating systems, and virtualization. I am now building a home lab to practice these skills in a hands-on environment.

Key Learning Areas

  • Command-line tools (Bash, PowerShell, Git)
  • Operating systems (Windows, Linux)
  • Networking fundamentals
  • Scripting and automation basics
  • Virtualization and home lab setup
  • Version control with Git and GitHub

What I Am Working Toward

My immediate goal is to land a help desk or junior system administrator position. From there, I plan to continue building skills in Linux, automation, containerization, and cloud infrastructure, with the long-term goal of moving into a DevOps role.

Outside of IT

When I am not studying or working on my home lab, I enjoy reading about technology, watching tutorials, and experimenting with new tools. I believe consistent practice and documentation are the best ways to learn.
